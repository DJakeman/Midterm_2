Roadmap to LPC

Credentials

  • Earn 60 semester credit hours from a board approved counseling program. Otherwise, you can earn 90 quarter credit hours in a counseling.
  • These hours include internship programs.
  • The counseling board will also accept programs that have been approved by the Counseling and Related Educational Programs (CACREP)

Core Courses

  • Human Growth and Development
  • Abnormal Human Behavior
  • Appraisal and Assessment techniques
  • Counseling Theories
  • Ethics and Professional Orientation
  • Research
  • Practicum and Internships -This requires at least 300 clock hours in counseling under supervision

Electives

  • Must have three credit semester hours or four quarter credit hours of at least five elective courses from group dynamics, lifestyle and career development, social and cultural foundations, addictions counseling, rehabilitation counseling, counseling with children and adolescents, gerontology, human sexuality, personality theories, crisis intervention, marriage and family counseling, clinical supervision, psychopharmacology

Licenses

  • The Oklahoma Board of Behavioral Health Licensure also provides information on becoming a Licensed Marital and Family Counselor, and a Licensed Behavioral Practitioner. However, both licenses require different required courses.
  • Prior to being full licensed the candidate can get a provisional license for up to three years.
  • LPC’s Can become LPC supervisors after completing a minimum of three lock hours of continuing education in counseling supervision during each renewal period. It requires a renewal every five years.

Ethics

  • Oklahoma’s ethical codes and regulations are called Okla. Admin. Code 310:405-3-2. This regulation governs LPC’s and LPC candidates.
  • After reviewing this regulation, it is apparent that the state of Oklahoma has adopted the ACA code of ethics. The regulation mirrors many aspects of the ACA (2014).

Advocacy and Training

  • The Oklahoma Counseling Association (OCA) is one many of the associations that the Oklahoma state Board of behavioral Health and Licensure recognizes.
  • The provide oppurtunities for LPC’s to get continuing education credits (CEUs) and they provide workshops for couselors to stay up to date with changes in the counseling community
  • LPC's are required to complete a minimum of 40 hours of CEU between the initial date of license and the expiration of the license for an initial renewal and for an annual renewal the LPC is required to complete 20 hours of CEU including three hours of an ethics CEU.
  • As a LPC supervisor, the supervisor will need to complete and addtional three credit hour CEU for counseling supervision
  • The OCA has a public policy issue that was reported by Healthy Minds Policy Initiative. The report states that health care memebers of various medicaid and medicare plans are forced to wait long periods of time before getting mental health treatment. Insurance plans do not show the majority of providers that accept them and many providers that do appear are unreachable or unavailable.
